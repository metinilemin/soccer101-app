🎓 SOCCER 101
8 rules
🇺🇸
No Shame Zone
Football explained using sports you already know. Every rule has a diagram + an NFL, NBA, or MLB analogy.
1
🚩 Offsides — the rule everyone gets wrong
▼
When the ball is passed forward to you, at least one defender (not the keeper) must be between you and the goal. If you're past all of them at the moment the ball is played — the flag goes up. You can't camp in front of the goal waiting.
🏀
NBA analogy: Like an offensive player not being allowed to stand in the paint waiting for a lob pass unless a defender is also there with them.
⚡
VAR reviews offsides to the millimeter. Armpit and shoulder positions have overturned World Cup goals.
2
🎯 Penalty Kick — purest pressure in sport
▼
If a foul happens inside the penalty box, the opposing team gets a penalty kick. One player, one keeper, 12 yards, one shot. No pressure at all. Except the entire nation watching. Used in shootouts to decide tied knockout games after 120 minutes.
🏈
NFL analogy: Imagine deciding the Super Bowl with a one-on-one field goal vs just the holder. After playing for 2 hours. That's a penalty shootout.
⚡
England lost in penalty shootouts in 1990, 1996, 1998, and 2006. At this point it's just tradition.
3
🚩 Corner Kick — chaos in the box
▼
When the ball crosses the goal line and was last touched by a defender, the attacking team gets a corner kick from the corner of the field. They'll cross the ball into the penalty box hoping for a headed goal. Causes pure chaos.
🏀
NBA analogy: Like an inbound play from the corner, but instead of 5 seconds, you get a free kick into a crowd of 10 players all jumping for the ball.
⚡
About 2–3% of corners result in a direct goal. But the chaos they create causes many indirect goals.
4
🟨🟥 Yellow & Red Cards — the punishment system
▼
Yellow card = official warning for a foul, time-wasting, or diving. Two yellows in the same match = automatic red. Red card = immediate ejection, no replacement. Your team plays the rest of the match with 10 players vs 11 — a massive disadvantage that often decides games.
🏒
NHL analogy: Like a power play penalty — except it lasts the whole rest of the game and you can never get the player back.
⚡
Zinedine Zidane headbutted a player in the 2006 World Cup FINAL and received a red card. France lost. He is still the most celebrated player in French history.
5
🏃 Free Kick — direct & indirect
▼
Awarded when a foul occurs outside the penalty box. Direct free kick = can shoot straight at goal. Indirect = must touch another player first. The defensive team builds a "wall" of players at least 10 yards away to block the shot.
🏈
NFL analogy: Like a field goal attempt — but the opposing team can put a human wall between you and the goalposts. You have to kick around or over it.
⚡
Cristiano Ronaldo has scored 50+ free kick goals in his career. His signature knuckleball technique makes it move unpredictably in the air.
6
🧤 The Goalkeeper — the one with hands
▼
The goalkeeper is the only player allowed to use their hands — but only inside their own penalty box. The moment they step outside the box and touch the ball with their hands, it's a foul and a direct free kick for the opponent.
🏀
NBA analogy: Like having one player who can catch and throw the ball — but only in the paint. The second they step outside the three-point line, they have to dribble like everyone else.
⚡
The goalkeeper jersey is a different color from both teams so the referee can always identify them instantly. Usually bright green or yellow — intentionally ugly.
7
🤲 Throw-In — the only time outfield players touch the ball
▼
When the ball crosses the sideline (touchline), the opposing team gets a throw-in. Must be thrown with both hands, over the head, with both feet on the ground. The thrower cannot touch the ball again until another player touches it first.
🏈
NFL analogy: Like an out-of-bounds restart — except instead of the ref spotting the ball, a player physically throws it back into play using very specific rules.
⚡
Some players can throw the ball over 40 meters — essentially a long pass. It's a trained skill, not just a restart.
8
👟 Goal Kick — restarting from the back
▼
When the attacking team kicks the ball over the goal line (but no goal), the defending team gets a goal kick. Usually taken by the goalkeeper from the six-yard box. All opponents must be outside the penalty box until the ball is kicked.
⚾
MLB analogy: Like a pitcher's free pass to reset — the goalkeeper gets a free kick to restart play and distribute the ball. It's often the start of a team's attack.
⚡
Modern goalkeepers are trained like field players — some of the most accurate long-range kickers in the game wear gloves.
